Keynote Title: Learning for Logical Reasoning and Low-level Motion Planning in Robotics

Task and motion planning (TAMP) problems in robotics are an instance of methods that combine logical reasoning and low-level motion planning, to enable complex, multi-step tasks. TAMP solvers typically combine symbolic planning over discrete task variables and abstractions of the state with motion optimization over continuous state and action variables, resulting in trajectories that satisfy the logical constraints imposed on the task variables. Despite significant progress in TAMP solvers over the last decade, several core problems remain open, one of them being that it is not clear what the role of learning should be to improve them. In this talk, we will show how to design TAMP solvers that learn to plan better and faster from past experience of solving similar problems in similar environments. We will show how a learning-based TAMP system can plan long-horizon manipulation tasks in various environments, including in chemistry labs, where robot arms can execute a diverse range of multi-step liquid handling experiments. #robotics #machinelearning #keynote #conference #computerscience #logicalreasoningOne of the most successful UTMIST projects -ğŸ”¥WallStreetBots: Crypto Price PredictionğŸ”¥- will deliver a keynote at EigenAI Conference by Project Director Lisa Yu, Final Year Computer Science Specialist student with Focus in AI and a Math Major at University of Toronto.
